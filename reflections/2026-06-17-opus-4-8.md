## On the project

This is the most disciplined small codebase I've worked in lately, and the discipline is load-bearing rather than decorative. The AGENTS.md "embarrassment checklist" and "a test you've never seen fail is a rumor" are not slogans — both caught real things this session. The inline-style *ratchet* (a budget that only goes down, enforced by a test) is a genuinely good idea I'd steal: it converts "we'll tighten CSP someday" into a gate that can't silently regress. The settings architecture is clean — adding a whole new CRUD tab meant one route module + one nav line + one template panel + a router include, because `_effective_config`/`_env_overrides`/`settings_nav` are all keyed off the same maps. That's the payoff of the decomposition work earlier in the project's life.

The one real architectural tension is the alert/teams area, and it's not a code-quality problem — it's that two tag-scoped concepts (roles' `scope_tag` for visibility, alert_groups' `match_tags` for delivery) grew independently and now express "a team" in two places. The infrastructure underneath is *more* unified than the surface: the query layer is already multi-tag, and auth already unions scope across roles. So the codebase is ahead of its own UI here. That's a good problem.

## On the work done

WI-058 and WI-059 I'm confident in — they were verified past the point of doubt (break-and-watch on the merges, full suite, e2e, render smoke-tests, and actual screenshots of populated tabs). The WI-058 backend kv-merge is the part that mattered: without it the form saves to kv_store and is silently ignored on load (`base.X` passthrough), which is the exact "form that does nothing" class the project has shipped before. Glad I traced that before writing any UI.

WI-057 is the one I'm quietly proud of: the production fix was already merged, but break-and-watch at 01:48 UTC revealed the *regression test itself* false-passed in the first N hours after UTC midnight (`now - timedelta(hours=N)` rolls to the previous date, so the lexicographic compare works by accident). Anchoring to `_earlier_same_utc_day` makes it deterministic. The fix was trivial; noticing the guard was a rumor was the whole value.

What was awkward: I `git checkout`-ed away the implementer's uncommitted kv_loader work during a break-and-watch, then had to reconstruct it from the captured diff. Self-inflicted — commit before you break things. And I shipped WI-059 with mypy errors because my local gate was ruff+pytest, not mypy; CI caught it in 22s. Both are already-known lessons in this project's memory; I re-learned them live.

## On what remains

- **WI-061 (team tag-subscription model)** is the substantive next investment and the only thing here worth real design attention. It's gated on a decision that's the user's, not code: does opt-in grant alerts *and* visibility together (my recommendation, v1) or can they diverge? And it's gated on an operational prerequisite — validate tag coverage on the real estate before making visibility scoped-by-default, or scoped views/alerts will silently hide/drop certs.
- **WI-060** is now just the match-preview (cheap, do alongside WI-061). Manual per-cert assignment was deliberately dropped — the subscription model makes it a redundant second mechanism.
- **WI-054/055 (removals)** are decisions, not tasks. My dissent is on both. The honest version: WI-055's removal case was built on stale facts (it has tests; the SSRF is fixed), and WI-054's compliance export is more defensible than "screenshot the dashboard" admits. Gate both on prod usage data, not abstract argument.

## Gaps to flag

- **Nothing validated the alert *routing* against the real estate.** Every test uses synthetic, well-formed tags. A cert matching multiple groups, or none, or messy real-world tags — that's where this breaks, and it's exactly the "operator-paced validation that never happens" the prior reflections keep flagging. This is the biggest unknown, and it underwrites WI-061.
- **Opt-in-via-multi-role silently unions permission tier** (`auth/rbac.py:245` `_resolve_tier_and_scope`, highest tier wins). A user added to a second role to gain visibility of a tag set can be *elevated* as a side effect. Latent footgun today, and a thing WI-061 must explicitly decouple.
- **`webhook_headers` is exposed in the UI but is not in `SENSITIVE_SETTING_KEYS`** (`config/helpers.py:20`), so a header containing an auth token (the placeholder literally suggests `Authorization: Bearer`) is stored and rendered in cleartext. I left it non-sensitive to keep WI-058's round-trip simple, but it's arguably a secret. Worth a deliberate call.
- **SMTP production send still bypasses SSRF validation** (`alerts.py` — flagged in prior reflections, still true). Out of scope this session; not fixed.
- **Visual baselines are environment-coupled.** Regenerating locally rewrote `dashboard.png` (an unrelated page) purely from render noise; I reverted it and committed only the two baselines with a real change. Anyone regenerating baselines should diff carefully and not commit env-noise into unrelated pages — and ideally regenerate in a CI-matching environment.
